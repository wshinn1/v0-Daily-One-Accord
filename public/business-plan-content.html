Biblical Foundation & Theological Rationale

Daily One Accord exists to serve the Church with tools that reflect biblical principles of unity, order, stewardship, shepherding care, and mission.

1) Unity that strengthens the Body

Jesus prays, "that they may all be one" (John 17:21). A unified platform helps leaders, teams, and members move together—reducing silos and strengthening fellowship (Acts 2:42–47; 1 Cor. 12).

What this means for the product: One place for communication, service planning, and follow-up so ministries operate as one, not as disconnected parts.

2) Order that frees people to serve

"Let all things be done decently and in order" (1 Cor. 14:40). Good systems don't replace the Spirit's work—they remove friction so people can serve with clarity.

What this means for the product: Shared calendars, service rundowns, roles/permissions, and repeatable workflows that reduce confusion and last-minute chaos (Acts 6:1–7 shows the early church organizing care so the Word could spread).

3) Stewardship that multiplies resources

"It is required of stewards that they be found faithful" (1 Cor. 4:2); the parable of the talents commends wise multiplication (Matt. 25:14–30).

What this means for the product: Affordable pricing, automation to save time, and analytics that guide wise decisions—helping churches do more ministry with fewer tools and lower costs (Prov. 27:23 "know well the condition of your flocks").

4) Shepherding care that doesn't lose people

Jesus pursues the one (Luke 15). Pastors are called to "shepherd the flock of God" (1 Pet. 5:2).

What this means for the product: Visitor pipelines, follow-up reminders, attendance insights, and notes—so people are known, contacted, and cared for, not lost between spreadsheets and apps.

5) Mission and discipleship made practical

The Great Commission calls us to make disciples (Matt. 28:19–20), which requires teaching, gathering, and sending with intention.

What this means for the product: Tools for classes, teams, communications, and events—so discipleship rhythms are scheduled, measurable, and repeatable.

6) Mercy, justice, and integrity

Pure religion includes caring for the vulnerable (Jas. 1:27) and walking in integrity (Prov. 11:3).

What this means for the product: Privacy-first design, role-based access, and auditability. Sensitive data is safeguarded; communication honors dignity.

How These Principles Shape Daily One Accord

  • Unified communication → minimizes confusion, encourages one-anothering (John 13:34–35).
  • Clear roles & permissions → equips the whole body to serve (Eph. 4:11–16).
  • Automated follow-up → pursues people with consistent care (Luke 15).
  • Transparent metrics → supports faithful stewardship and planning (1 Cor. 4:2).
  • Affordable pricing → frees resources for mission and mercy (Acts 4:32–35).

Ministry Posture (Guardrails)

  • People over process: Tools support pastoral wisdom; they don't replace it.
  • Privacy & dignity: Sensitive data is protected; communications honor people.
  • Simplicity first: We reduce noise so leaders can focus on presence and prayer (Acts 6:4).
  • Kingdom impact: Success is measured by faithfulness, fruit, and care—not vanity metrics.

2. The Problem

The Hidden Cost of Fragmented Church Communication

Most churches don't have a communication problem—they have a FRAGMENTATION problem.

Picture this: Your worship leader posts the service plan in Slack. Your admin emails it to volunteers. Your pastor texts last-minute changes. Your tech team checks three different places to find the right information. By Sunday morning, nobody's on the same page.

This isn't just inefficient—it's organizationally destructive.

The Fragmentation Crisis

Churches today operate with 5-10 disconnected tools:

  • Slack for team chat (but no task tracking)
  • Email for "official" communication (that nobody reads)
  • Text messages for urgent updates (that get lost in threads)
  • Planning Center for scheduling (that volunteers don't check)
  • Google Drive for files (that nobody can find)
  • Separate giving platform (that doesn't talk to anything else)

The Real Cost:

1. Communication Breakdown

  • Staff spend 15-20 hours/week coordinating across platforms
  • Critical information gets lost between systems
  • Teams operate in silos, unaware of what others are doing
  • Volunteers miss updates because they're in the "wrong" channel

2. Zero Accountability

  • No single source of truth for who's responsible for what
  • Tasks fall through the cracks between platforms
  • Follow-up becomes impossible when data lives in 7 different places
  • Leadership can't see the full picture of ministry operations

3. Growth Paralysis

  • New staff/volunteers face a learning curve across multiple tools
  • Scaling requires adding MORE tools, creating MORE fragmentation
  • Decision-making slows to a crawl as teams hunt for information
  • Ministry momentum dies in the gap between disconnected systems

4. Financial Drain

  • $5,000-$20,000 annually on fragmented subscriptions
  • Hidden costs: Slack ($840-$26,250/year), email tools, SMS platforms
  • Staff burnout from coordination overhead
  • Lost giving opportunities from poor visitor follow-up

The Bottom Line

Fragmented communication doesn't just waste time—it prevents growth. When your team spends more time coordinating than executing, ministry suffers. When information lives in silos, accountability disappears. When systems don't talk to each other, your church can't scale.

Daily One Accord solves the fragmentation crisis by creating ONE unified platform where communication, coordination, and accountability happen in real-time.

📅 Annual Maintenance Fee: $99/year

Starting Year 2, all church tenants pay a $99 annual maintenance fee.

What It Covers:

  • Ongoing Support: Technical support, bug fixes, and system maintenance
  • Infrastructure Costs: Covers inflation and price increases from third-party services (Vercel, Supabase, Telnyx, etc.)
  • Platform Updates: Security patches, performance improvements, and minor feature enhancements
  • Operational Stability: Ensures long-term sustainability without raising monthly subscription prices

When It's Due:

  • Year 1: No maintenance fee (covered by setup fee)
  • Year 2+: $99 annual fee due on the anniversary of your initial setup fee payment
  • Example: If you paid your $199 setup fee on January 15, 2025, your first $99 maintenance fee is due January 15, 2026

Why This Model Works:

By separating maintenance costs from monthly subscriptions, we can:

  • Keep monthly subscription prices locked in forever (grandfathered pricing)
  • Cover rising operational costs without penalizing early adopters
  • Maintain financial sustainability as the platform scales
  • Provide transparent, predictable pricing for churches

What Are "Seats" in Daily One Accord?

Seats = Number of Slack users per church

How It Works:

  1. Your Church Sets Up Slack
    • Each church creates their own Slack workspace (free or paid)
    • You pay Slack directly for your Slack subscription
    • You control your Slack workspace and user management
  2. Daily One Accord Integrates
    • We connect to your existing Slack workspace
    • Our platform sends notifications, updates, and messages to your Slack channels
    • Your team uses Slack as the communication hub for all church operations
  3. You Pay for Seats
    • Count how many people in your church use Slack
    • Choose the plan that covers your seat count
    • No per-seat charges within your plan's range

Appendix: Security and Compliance Documentation

Security Architecture

Daily One Accord implements enterprise-grade security measures that exceed industry standards for church management software.

Infrastructure Security

  • Hosting: Vercel's global edge network with automatic DDoS protection
  • Database: Supabase (enterprise PostgreSQL) with automatic backups
  • SSL/TLS: All connections encrypted with modern cipher suites
  • Uptime: 99.9% SLA with multi-region redundancy

Application Security

  • Row Level Security (RLS): Database-level access control on every query
  • Authentication: Supabase Auth with OAuth 2.0 and JWT tokens
  • Password Hashing: Bcrypt with salt (industry standard)
  • Session Management: Secure, expiring tokens with automatic refresh
  • CSRF Protection: Built-in cross-site request forgery prevention
  • XSS Prevention: Input sanitization and output encoding

Data Protection

  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data
  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 for all network communication
  • Backup Encryption: All backups encrypted with separate keys
  • Key Management: Secure key rotation and storage

Access Controls

  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Five permission levels
  • Granular Permissions: Control view, edit, delete at feature level
  • Audit Logging: All data access and changes logged
  • IP Whitelisting: Available on Enterprise tier
  • Multi-Factor Authentication: Planned for Q2 2025

Compliance

  • GDPR: Full compliance with European data protection regulations
  • CCPA: California Consumer Privacy Act compliance
  • COPPA: Children's Online Privacy Protection Act considerations
  • SOC 2: Planned certification for 2025

Monitoring and Incident Response

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Sentry integration for error tracking
  • Security Alerts: Automated alerts for suspicious activity
  • Incident Response Plan: Documented procedures for security events
  • Penetration Testing: Annual third-party security audits

Data Ownership and Portability

  • Church Owns Data: Clear contractual ownership
  • Export Capabilities: Download all data in CSV, JSON, or SQL formats
  • Data Deletion: Complete data removal upon request
  • No Data Mining: We never sell or share church data

Security Commitment

We treat church data with the same level of security as financial institutions. Our security practices are transparent, regularly audited, and continuously improved. Churches can trust that their sensitive member information is protected with enterprise-grade security measures.
