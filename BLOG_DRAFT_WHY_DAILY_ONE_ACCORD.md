## The Problem: Church Fragmentation

Most churches today are drowning in disconnected tools. The average church uses 5-10 different platforms just to keep things running:

- **Communication breakdown**: Messages scattered across email, text, Slack, and social media
- **Zero accountability**: No way to track who's doing what or if tasks are getting done
- **Growth paralysis**: Can't scale ministry because systems don't talk to each other
- **Financial drain**: Paying $200-500/month for tools that don't integrate

This fragmentation doesn't just cost money—it costs mission effectiveness. When your team spends more time managing tools than ministering to people, something's broken.

## The Solution: Unity in One Platform

Daily One Accord was built on a simple but powerful idea: **What if everything your church needs lived in one place?**

Not just "integrated"—truly unified. One login. One dashboard. One source of truth.

## The Founder's Story

I'm Wes Shinn, and my journey to creating Daily One Accord started in an unexpected place: military photojournalism.

For over two decades, I documented impactful stories and captured intricate details during major national disasters. This experience taught me to remain calm under pressure and find beauty in challenging situations.

With 17 years as a professional photographer and filmmaker, and formal education in film school, I've mastered the art of transforming moments into lasting memories. My cinematic eye and passion for storytelling have served hundreds of couples and families.

But it was in ministry where I saw the real problem.

Churches were using 5-10 different tools, none of which talked to each other. Pastors were spending hours each week just managing software instead of shepherding people. Volunteers were confused about schedules. Communication was falling through the cracks.

I realized: **The church needs what I learned in crisis situations—calm, clarity, and systems that work under pressure.**

That's why I built Daily One Accord.
